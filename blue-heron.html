Blue Heron

Since moving to the lake, I've spent hours watching Great Blue Herons glide overhead like modern-day dinosaurs. They carry themselves with such absolute royalty.

These two sculptures are a love letter to these frequent lakeside visitors, specifically celebrating their stunning physical transformation during mating season. I wanted to highlight the fleeting, vibrant glow they get when looking for a mate:

The Lores: The skin around their eyes turns a striking, bright cobalt blue.

The Bill: Their dagger-like bill shifts from dull yellow to a burning red-orange.

The Plumes: Elaborate feathers on the neck and breast with deep blues, grays and white.
